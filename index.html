About

Alien is series of movies beginning in 1979. There are four films that follow eachother: Alien(1979), Aliens(1986), Alien 3(1992), and Alien Resurrection(1997). Though most fans and critics say that the last two films in the series are the worst, they add to the lore that is within the Alien community.

Alien (1979), starts off with the crew of the USCSS Nostromo, an M-Class star freighter set on course to Earth while towing a mineral ore refinery. The crew (Dallas, Kane, Ripley, Ash, Lambert, Parker, and Brett) awaken to find out that their ships interface MU/TH/ER, has intercepted a distress signal from an unknown source on an uninhabited moon. Dallas, Kane and Lambert go to check the distress beacon and Kane ends up with an alien organism attached to his face. The team go back to the ship (in complete disregard to Ripley and the science quarantine law) and end up infecting the ship with a stray Xenomorph. It begins wreaking havoc and killing off the crew members one by one until Ripley initiates the self destruct sequence on the ship and escapes on the emergency escape shuttle, the Narcissus.

Aliens (1986) takes place 57 years after the original movie. Ripley is rescued by a salvage team after slipping through the network while in hypersleep. She was taken to the Gateway Station (a medical facility) and met Carter Burke, an employee representing Weyland-Yutani. Since being discovered, Ripley needs to defend herself at the legal case for the destruction of the Nostromo. However, the executives of Weyland-Yutani's legal team don't believe her story of the Xenomorph and strip her of her license. Some time later, she is informed that the military has lost contact with the terraforming colony on LV-426. She decides to join Burkes and a crew of soldiers to the facility to see what happened. They discover that there has in fact been a Xenomorph outbreak and many of the colonists are dead. Ripley finds a young girl named Newt and looks after her for the rest of the movie. The soldiers find the main hive of Xenomorphs, but are ambushed and many of them die while Burke and Ripley watch through their helmet cameras. The remaining survivors camp out inside the colony complex and barricade as well has set up sentry guns to keep the Xenomorphs at bay. Ripley discovers that Burke ordered the colonists to investigate the derelict spaceship and bring back specimens. She threatons to expose him. Ripley and Newt later wake up in the med lab only to find a couple facehuggers that Burke had set loose inside. The marines threaten to execute Burke after saving Ripley and Newt, only to be interrupted by a power loss caused by the Xenomorphs. Everyone gathers supplies, ammo, and weapons, to defend their area. As the Xenomorphs get closer and closer the tracker suggests that they are in the room with them. One marine opens the celing panel and begins shooting up, leading everyone else to do the same. Ripley and Newt escape through the vents while everyone else follows. Multiple marines are killed through the vents, and Newt is later kidnapped by a Xenomorph. While waiting for their transportation, Ripley decides to go to the Xenomorph nest and rescue Newt, setting it on fire in the process. As they are about to board the ship, Ripley realized that the queen Xenomorph had followed them and leads it to the airlock, killing it.

Alien 3 (1992), follows Ripley yet again on her quest with the Xenomorph. This movie starts out with a great way to make the audience mad, when they reveal that the shuttle (the USS Sulaco) that Ripley, Newt, Bishop and Hicks escaped to had crash landed on Fiorina "Fury" 161. A dense planet populated solely by male prisoners who are devoted to celibacy. Viewers learn that a fire had caused the Sulaco to land, killing everyone on board except Ripley. She later asks the prisoner doctor to perform an autopsy on Newt, specifically to cut open her chest, and make absolutely sure there were no organisms inside of her. Even though there wasn't anything inside of Newt, a facehugger survived on the Sulaco and attacked one of the prisoners salvaging the ship. This leads to the Xenomorph growing and running around the prison picking people off one by one. Ripley encounters a close-up encounter with a Xenomorph, and becomes confused when it decides not to kill her. Later on she realizes it is because she had a queen implanted in her from the queen at the end of Aliens while she was in hypersleep. Ripley eventually aids in the death of the Xenomorph by having a prisoner lead it to the metal pouring area, and pouring molton lead on it. This doesn't work entirely so Ripley runs to turn on the fire sprinklers, which crack the Xenomorph's exoskeleton due to thermal shock. After she kills the Xenomorph, a Weyland-Yutani executive who modeled the Bishop android attempts to convince Ripley to let him have the Xenomorph queen growing inside of her. Ripley, knowing that Weyland-Yutani is going to keep the specimen and attempt to weaponize it or risk all life on Earth, decides to commit suicide by jumping into the pit of molton lead with the queen inside of her to officially kill it.

Alien Resurrection (1997) happens 200 years after Alien 3. Scientists have worked for years trying to clone Ripley's DNA in an attempt to obtain Xenomorph DNA. Eventually they are successful on the eighth attempt, creating Ripley 8. Because she was cloned from her previous DNA where she had a Xenomorph growing inside of her, she is a mix between human and Xenomorph thus giving her a massive amount of strength (as well as acidic blood). Eventually, as the same situation arises in the previous movies, the Xenomorph specimens have escaped and are picking off all of the scientists on the ship. Ripley 8 and a few other survivors leave and attempt to escape the ship. While leaving, Ripley 8 discovers that the scientists had used her Xenomorph DNA to clone a Xenomorph, however when it lays its spawn instead of an egg, its clear that the Xenomorph queen also has human DNA, as it lays organisms instead off eggs, and its spawn has human like features such as a human skull and bone structure, while also being a brutish Xenomorph. It manages to kill the queen in one strong hit, and walks over to Ripley 8. It acts as if Ripley 8 is its real mother and looks to her with a loving expression. One of the other survivors lures it away from Ripley 8 so she can escape with the rest of the crew. Later everyone meets up at the airlock inside of the escape betty where the humainoid Xenomorph just happens to be at. Ripley 8 is saddened with her realization that she needs to kill it. She calms it down by walking towards it after it attacks multiple survivors. After getting near it, she sees the airlock window and throws her acid blood onto it, causing it to suck the humanoid Xenomorph out the window, crushing it and sucking all of its blood and crushed bones out.
